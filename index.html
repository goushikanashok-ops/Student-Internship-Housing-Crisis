About this report

What we care about and why

This report investigates the financial, geographic, racial, and institutional barriers that prevent low-income students from accessing professional opportunities. We believe housing shouldn't determine who gets a career.

A NACE survey of 20,000 graduating seniors found that 52.5 percent completed internships during college — but outcomes split sharply by pay status. Paid interns had a 61 percent job offer rate after graduation. Unpaid interns had just 38 percent. That 23-point gap shows that the internship system doesn't just reward experience. It rewards the financial ability to work without pay.

"The system isn't rewarding talent. It's rewarding the ability to pay."

The problem

Why students can't afford to intern

Even when students land competitive internships, geography creates another barrier. Most high-value internships are clustered in cities like Los Angeles, where the map below shows a severe jobs-housing imbalance. Students who can't afford to live near the job center get pushed to the outer edges of the metro — adding costly, exhausting commutes on top of everything else. A cheaper apartment in Riverside doesn't mean a cheaper summer when you're paying for gas and transit every day.

A way forward

Structural fixes, not individual workarounds

Solving the internship housing crisis requires action from universities, employers, and policymakers together. Universities should create summer housing networks that connect students to available dorm space near their internship sites. Employers should provide commute scores, vetted housing lists, and transit support before interns sign a lease. At the base level, internships should be paid or fully funded — because no student should have to go into debt to build a resume.

01
Require paid or fully funded internships Universities should not allow students to take internships for credit unless the internship pays minimum wage or provides a stipend covering housing, food, and transportation.
02
Build a university-to-university housing network Use underused summer dorms so students can access affordable housing at partner campuses near their internship site.
03
Require employer commute scores Employers should publish realistic commute data — time, cost, safety, transit options — before interns sign a lease in an unfamiliar city.
04
Build a verified internship housing platform A centralized, scam-free platform with verified listings, lease guidance, and student reviews so no one starts from scratch.
05
Expand remote and hybrid options Remote internships reduce geographic barriers. Employers should treat them as full opportunities, not second-tier fallbacks.
