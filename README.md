Students' Internship Housing problem.
Created for the UCSD Warren College Writing Program, a website to house a policy report, 2026.
About
This project investigates the economic, geographical, racial and institutional obstacles to low-income students pursuing professional internships. The website is the on-line version of our think tank report, which promotes structural changes in internships to ensure that all students, regardless of financial status, are equally represented.

Key Arguments
The report covers four types of barriers, namely:

Financial — Unpaid internships are more expensive for students than paid internships, because they result in lost wages and living costs that are up to $12,986 for a summer session.
Geographic — internships are focused in high cost areas such as Los Angeles and New York where students are forced to live in areas away from job opportunities.
Housing discrimination — Racial discrimination: Black and Hispanic students are more likely to experience housing discrimination, and unpaid internship structures are more likely to exclude them
Institutional — No one has a single place or university assistance to guide students on house-hunting in new cities

The report suggests the following five solutions: Paid or funded internship standards; University-to-university summer housing network; Employer commute scores; A verified housing platform; Expanded remote/hybrid internship opportunities.
