# Student Internship Housing Crisis

A policy report website created for the UCSD Warren College Writing Program, 2026.

## About

This project examines the financial, geographic, racial, and institutional barriers that prevent low-income students from accessing professional internship opportunities. The site serves as the online presence for our think tank report, advocating for structural reforms to make internships equitable for all students regardless of financial background.

## Key Arguments

The report covers four categories of barriers:

1. **Financial** — Unpaid internships cost students up to $12,986 per summer in lost wages and living expenses, while producing weaker career outcomes than paid internships
2. **Geographic** — Internships concentrate in high-cost cities like Los Angeles and New York, pushing students into unaffordable housing far from job centers
3. **Racial** — Black and Hispanic students face higher rates of housing discrimination and are disproportionately excluded by unpaid internship structures
4. **Institutional** — No centralized platform or university support exists to help students navigate short-term housing searches in unfamiliar cities

The report proposes five solutions: paid or funded internship standards, a university-to-university summer housing network, employer commute scores, a verified housing platform, and expanded remote/hybrid internship options.
